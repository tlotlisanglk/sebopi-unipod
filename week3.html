Figue 1 below shows steps to design an alarm door that rings when the upper switch magnet is detached from the other magnet and become silent when the two magnets are in contact.

Figue 1 above shows the design of the door size. The front plane was selected so that you can sketch on it. The feature called center rectangle was used to draw the shape of the door by left clicking the mouse and dragging it so that you can enlarge or reduce the size of the rectangle.Another feature called smart dimension was used to give dimension size for the length and width of the door.

Figue 2 below shows steps to design an alarm door that rings when the upper switch magnet is detached from the other magnet and become silent when the two magnets are in contact.

Figue 2 above shows the extruded boss/bass feature where the rectangle sketch of the door was given the height in the z-direction. The process is to turn a 2D rectangle into a 3D shape. The direction height was 5mm and the direction 1 parameter was selected as Blind.

Figue 4 below shows steps to design an alarm door that rings when the upper switch magnet is detached from the other magnet and become silent when the two magnets are in contact.

Figue 4 above shows the design of a door lock. A feature called a straight slot was used to sketch the shape of the lock. Inside the shape of the lock I used the same feature to draw the area where the key will be used to deactivate the alram that was set. The other feature called the extruded cut was used so that there is a hole to insert the key.

Figue 5 below shows steps to design an alarm door that rings when the upper switch magnet is detached from the other magnet and become silent when the two magnets are in contact.

The part where a person will use his/her hands to twist open the lock was created using the feature called circle and was extruded to give the height. Smart dimension was used to give other dimensions of the door. Also, the straight slot was Used to sketch the other part of the handle door and extruded to give it a height.

Reflection

The lesson introduces the 3D design using Solid works. The design starts with the sketch of what you need to do and then you use the features like extrude to build the 3D model.
